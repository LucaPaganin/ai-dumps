Understanding the AI-102 Exam Blueprint

The AI-102 exam, officially "Skills Measured," has undergone significant revisions (effective April 30, 2025), emphasizing Generative AI, Azure AI Foundry (Azure AI Studio), and AI Agents. Understanding this blueprint is crucial for focused preparation. This section breaks down the key skill areas and their weightings. Click "✨ Explain" for AI-powered insights on each topic.

Distribution of skills measured in the AI-102 exam.

Key Skill Areas:

  • Plan and manage an Azure AI solution (20–25%)
  • Implement generative AI solutions (15–20%)
  • Implement an agentic solution (5–10%)
  • Implement computer vision solutions (10–15%)
  • Implement natural language processing solutions (15–20%)
  • Implement knowledge mining and information extraction solutions (15–20%)

Important Note on Revisions:

Significant updates emphasize Generative AI (Azure AI Foundry, Azure OpenAI Service) and a new section on AI Agents. The weightings for Computer Vision and NLP have decreased, while Generative AI and Knowledge Mining/Information Extraction have increased. Ensure your study materials reflect these changes.

Practice Question Resources

A variety of resources can help you prepare, from official Microsoft offerings to third-party paid options. This section highlights the most recommended free resources and discusses paid alternatives, emphasizing the need for current and relevant material.

Microsoft Official Resources (Free)

These are foundational, comprehensive, and always up-to-date:

  • Microsoft Learn: Extensive modules, learning paths, and hands-on labs. Universally recommended.
  • Official AI-102 Practice Test (from Microsoft): ~50 questions, aligns with current objectives.
  • Microsoft AI Engineer Exercises / Hands-on Labs (GitHub): Crucial for practical skills.
  • Exam Prep Video Series: Microsoft Reactor, John Savill (highly recommended), FreeCodeCamp offer valuable video content.

Paid Practice Question Providers

If seeking additional practice, consider these, but verify their alignment with the latest exam updates:

  • MeasureUp: Official Microsoft partner, generally high quality but pricier. Likely covers new topics.
  • Whizlabs: Mixed Reddit reviews. Verify content recency.
  • Udemy (e.g., Scott Duffy, Nahid Perween): Quality varies by instructor. Look for recently updated courses. Scott Duffy's is often praised.
  • Pluralsight / A Cloud Guru: Offers learning paths and sandbox environments.
  • Edusum / SkillCertPro: More affordable options, but reviews are mixed. Diligence needed.

Critical: The AI-102 exam has significantly changed. Ensure any paid resource explicitly covers Generative AI, AI Foundry, and AI Agents as per the post-April 2025 blueprint.

General Sentiment on Practice Questions

There's high demand for reliable practice tests. MeasureUp is respected but costly. Users often seek affordable, effective alternatives. Whizlabs and SkillCertPro get mixed reviews. Udemy quality varies by instructor and recency. Microsoft Learn is consistently lauded for comprehensive coverage.

Relevance to Actual Exam Content

Candidates seek tests that closely simulate the real exam. MeasureUp is often found comparable. Newer practice tests tend to focus on appropriate service use and UI navigation over obscure details. Recent exams include case studies and hands-on labs, so materials should reflect this. The exam is kept very up-to-date with Azure portal changes, making material recency critical.

Coverage of Learning Paths (including new topics)

Microsoft Learn is praised for covering all objectives. Some third-party materials, especially older Udemy courses, might lack coverage or recency. While OpenAI and Generative AI are emphasized, specific discussions on "Azure AI Foundry" or "AI Agents" coverage by providers are less common in analyzed feedback, likely due to their newness. The strong emphasis on up-to-date materials implies these new topics *must* be covered by reputable current resources.
